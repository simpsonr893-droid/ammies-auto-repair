# No Photographer? No Problem.

*Brand: Instant Camera for Events | Category: Event Planning*

---

A professional photographer costs between $1,500 and $4,000 for a full-day event. For a wedding, that's the floor. For a corporate event, a birthday party, a graduation celebration — it's often a line item that gets cut.

And when it gets cut, what usually happens is: twenty people take photos on their phones throughout the night, and six months later, nobody has organized them, nobody has shared them, and the best shots are trapped on a device that may or may not still be charged.

This isn't a photography quality problem. Modern phones take extraordinary photos. This is a coordination problem — getting all those good photos from all those individual phones into one place that everyone can access.

**Instant Camera for Events** doesn't replace a photographer. It solves the coordination problem.

## How it works at a real event

Before the event: you open the app, set the event name. Every photo taken will have that name burned in — no sorting required later.

During the event: guests use it on their own phones, no download needed. They can pick their own filter, flip to the front camera for selfies, use the countdown timer for group shots. The experience is fast enough that they'll actually use it.

After the event: the photos are already on everyone's devices, already labeled, already shareable. The gallery built itself while everyone was having fun.

## What you actually get

- **Coverage from every angle** — not just wherever one photographer was standing
- **Authentic moments** — guests photograph what matters to them, not what looks good for the portfolio
- **Instant sharing** — photos go out in real time, not two weeks after the event when the energy is gone
- **A consistent look** — one filter, set once, applied to every photo from every guest

## The honest truth about professional photography

Professional photography is worth it for the moments that absolutely have to be perfect. The first look. The exchange of rings. The formal family portrait. These are the photos that live in frames for thirty years and you need them to be right.

But a professional photographer cannot be in the kitchen when the caterers are plating the food. They cannot be at the bar when two old friends run into each other. They cannot be at table seven when the best man says something that makes everyone lose it.

Your guests can.

Give them the tool that makes it easy. They'll cover everything the professional doesn't — and the two sets of photos together will tell the full story of the day.
