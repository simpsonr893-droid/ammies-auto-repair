# How to Set Up a Digital Photo Booth for Your Next Event (No Hardware Required)

*Brand: Instant Camera for Events | Type: How-To Guide | Target: DIY Event Planners*

---

## Introduction

A traditional photo booth setup involves renting a hardware unit, arranging delivery and pickup, managing a vendor contract, and hoping the equipment works on the day. Costs typically range from $800 to $2,500 for a single event.

A digital photo booth using **Instant Camera for Events** costs nothing, requires no delivery, and can be set up in under ten minutes. This guide walks through exactly how to do it.

---

## What You Need

- The Instant Camera for Events URL
- A way to display or distribute that URL to guests
- That's it

No camera. No printer. No iPad kiosk. No vendor.

---

## Step 1: Set Your Event Name

Before the event, open [instant-camera-for-events.rork.app](https://instant-camera-for-events.rork.app) on any device and set the event name. Every photo taken through the app will have this name burned directly into the image.

Choose a name that is specific enough to be meaningful but short enough to read clearly in the corner of a photo:
- "Smith Wedding 2025" ✓
- "Q3 Sales Kickoff" ✓
- "Maya's 30th Birthday Party at the Rooftop Bar on June 14th" ✗ (too long)

---

## Step 2: Choose a Default Filter

You can leave the filter selection to individual guests, or — if you want a fully consistent look across all photos — you can set a recommended filter in your guest instructions.

**Recommendations by event type:**
- Black tie / formal events → B&W or Vintage
- Outdoor summer events → Vivid or Warm
- Corporate / conference → Normal or Cool
- Rustic / barn venues → Sepia or Faded
- Nighttime / nightclub → Cool or Vivid

---

## Step 3: Distribute the Link

This is the most important step. The more access points you create, the more photos your guests will take.

### QR Codes at Tables
Generate a QR code from the URL (any free QR code generator works). Print it on table cards, menus, or photo placards. Place one at every table and at the bar.

### Event Welcome Email
Include the link in your pre-event communication with a one-line explanation: "Use this to take event photos — they'll be automatically labeled and ready to share."

### Venue Screens
If your venue has display screens, add a slide with the QR code and a short instruction. Rotate it into the display loop throughout the event.

### Day-Of Group Chat
If your event has a guest group chat or a venue-specific messaging channel, drop the link there with a brief note.

### Printed Signage
A 5x7 or 8x10 printed card at the entrance with the QR code and event name is the simplest physical distribution method.

---

## Step 4: Brief Your Guests (One Sentence)

Guests don't need a tutorial, but a single sentence of context helps. The most effective framing:

> "Scan this to take photos tonight — they'll all be stamped with [Event Name] and easy to share."

That's the whole briefing. The app handles the rest.

---

## Step 5: Let It Run

You don't need to manage the photo booth during the event. There's no hardware to monitor, no paper to reload, no tech support calls to handle. Guests use it on their own devices on their own timeline.

If you want to encourage usage at a specific moment — group photo time, the cake cutting, the end of the night — a brief verbal announcement ("Everyone grab your phones and use the camera link for this one") is sufficient.

---

## Step 6: After the Event

Photos taken through Instant Camera for Events are stored locally on each guest's device and shared in real time through whatever channels they chose. There's no central server to log into, no album to compile.

What you will find, if you monitor your event hashtag or branded search term, is a collection of consistently labeled photos distributed across social platforms — organic content that extends your event's reach without any post-event effort on your part.
