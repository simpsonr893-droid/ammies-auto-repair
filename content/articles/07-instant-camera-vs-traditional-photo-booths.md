## Coverage

### Traditional Photo Booth
- Fixed location — guests must physically go to the booth
- Coverage is linear — one group at a time
- Guests at the back of the venue, deep in conversation, or late arrivals often never visit
- Average utilization: 30–50% of guests in typical event settings
- Coverage is concentrated in posed, staged shots

### Instant Camera for Events
- Distributed — every guest's phone is a camera station
- Coverage is parallel — hundreds of guests can photograph simultaneously
- Every corner of the venue, every spontaneous moment is accessible
- Usage rate tracks distribution of the URL — higher when QR codes are present at every table
- Coverage includes both posed and candid shots

**Verdict:** Instant Camera for Events provides fundamentally broader coverage. A single hardware unit cannot compete with distributed access across an entire guest list.

## Photo Quality

### Traditional Photo Booth
- Fixed camera hardware (quality varies by vendor)
- Controlled lighting (typically studio-quality flash setup)
- Consistent framing (fixed background)
- Prints available on-site (4x6 or strip format)
- Digital copies usually delivered via email link post-event

### Instant Camera for Events
- Camera quality is the guest's own device hardware (modern flagship phones exceed most booth cameras)
- Lighting is ambient — quality varies by venue and position
- Framing is guest-controlled
- Digital output only — no prints
- Photos available instantly on device and shareable in real time

**Verdict:** For controlled, posed shots with on-site prints, traditional booths have an advantage. For overall image quality ceiling and real-time availability, Instant Camera for Events leverages better hardware (modern phone cameras) with the trade-off of no print output.

## Guest Experience

### Traditional Photo Booth
- Dedicated experience — guests know they're "doing the photo booth"

- Queue creates a social gathering point (positive or negative depending on wait times)
- Requires guests to find and go to the booth
- Novelty has declined as photo booths have become ubiquitous

### Instant Camera for Events
- Integrated into normal phone behavior — lower activation energy
- Can be used anywhere, anytime during the event
- No queue, no wait
- Filters and event name add novelty within a familiar interface
- Works equally well for solo shots, candids, and group photos

**Verdict:** Traditional booths create a higher-intensity, more social focal point. Instant Camera for Events creates lower-friction, higher-frequency photo behavior throughout the event.
